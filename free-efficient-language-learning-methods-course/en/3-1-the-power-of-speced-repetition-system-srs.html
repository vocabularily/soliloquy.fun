Module 3: The Memory Machine - Vocabulary & Grammar a Better Way

The Power of Spaced Repetition Systems (SRS)

Have you ever crammed for a test, only to forget everything a week later? This phenomenon is explained by something called the "Forgetting Curve," discovered by psychologist Hermann Ebbinghaus.

The Forgetting Curve: When you learn something new, your memory of it decays exponentially. You might forget 50% of it within an hour, and 90% within a week if you don't review it.

The Solution: Spaced Repetition. What if you could review that new piece of information at the exact moment your brain was about to forget it? Doing so sends a powerful signal to your brain: "This is important! Keep it." Each time you do this, the memory becomes stronger, and the time until you next need to review it gets longer and longer (e.g., 1 day, 4 days, 10 days, 1 month, 4 months, etc.).

This is the principle of spaced repetition. But how can you possibly track when to review thousands of words? You don't have to. Software does it for you.

The Tool: Spaced Repetition Systems (SRS)

An SRS is essentially a "smart" flashcard program. It's your personal memory machine. Here's how it works:

  1. You create a flashcard for a new word or phrase.
  2. The program shows you the front of the card. You try to remember the back.
  3. You then tell the program how challenging it was for you to remember (e.g., "Hard," "Good," or "Easy").
  4. Based on your rating, the SRS algorithm schedules the next time you'll see that card. Easy cards get pushed far into the future. Hard cards will come back soon.

This ensures you spend your precious study time efficiently, focusing only on the things you are on the verge of forgetting. It is the opposite of cramming. It is the key to remembering thousands of words with minimal effort.

Your 60-Minute Action Plan for Today

  1. (15 mins) Review this Lesson: Let the concepts of the Forgetting Curve and Spaced Repetition sink in. This explains why traditional studying can feel so futile and how we can fix it.
  2. (30 mins) Set Up Your SRS Tool: Your main task today is to build your memory machine. Download Soliloquy app.
  3. (15 mins) Create Your First 3 "Smart" Cards: Now, let's add a few cards from the words you discovered in Module 2
    • Click on "+Add flashcard"
    • The Golden Rule: Don't just add single words. Type in the whole short sentence you want to learn and then hit the translation button.
    • Repeat: Do this for 2–4 more words you logged in the last module.
  4. Prepare for Tomorrow: You now have the tool (Soliloquy) and know how to make a card. Our next lesson, "Building Your Core Vocabulary," will focus on the strategy of what you should be putting into your SRS to get the fastest results.

You have set up a system that will serve you for your entire language learning journey. This is a true game-changer.
