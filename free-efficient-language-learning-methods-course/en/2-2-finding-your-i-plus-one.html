Module 2: The Input Phase - Building Your Foundation

Finding Your "i+1"

Great job on taking that first step into the world of input! It can feel a bit strange at first to just listen without stressing over every word, but it's the most natural way to learn.

You might have felt that the material was either a little too easy or a little too hard. So, how do you find content that's perfectly tuned for your level? That's our mission for today.

This is a direct follow-up to Dr. Krashen's theory of Comprehensible Input. It's a simple formula that helps you find the perfect learning materials.

Let's break it down:

  • "i" = your current level. This represents all the vocabulary and grammar you currently know and understand in your target language. It's your comfort zone.
  • "+1" = the next step. This is the new information—a new word, a new phrase, a slightly more complex sentence structure—that is just one small step beyond your current abilities.

"i+1" is the sweet spot of language acquisition.

It's material that is firmly grounded in what you already know (the "i"), so you can understand the overall message, but it also contains a small amount of new information (the "+1") that you can figure out from the context.

An Analogy for "i+1":

  • Material that is just "i" (Too Easy): Imagine you know 100 words. You read a story that only uses those 100 words. It's 100% comprehensible, but it's not challenging. You aren't acquiring anything new.
  • Material that is "i + 100" (Too Hard): You know 100 words, and you try to read a newspaper article filled with thousands of unknown words. It's incomprehensible noise. Your brain can't find a foothold to learn anything.
  • Material that is "i + 1" (Just Right): You know 100 words. You read a sentence where you know 9 out of 10 words. You can use the context of the 9 words you know to easily guess the meaning of the 10th unknown word. Your brain absorbs it almost effortlessly. That is acquisition.

Your goal is to find materials where you comprehend roughly 70-95% of what's being said or written. You should be able to follow the main story or idea, but still notice some new words and structures along the way.

Your 60-Minute Action Plan for Today

  1. (10 mins) Review this Lesson: Internalize the concept of "i+1". Your new goal isn't just to find "input," but to find perfectly challenging input.
  2. (35 mins) The "i+1" Scavenger Hunt: Your mission is to find and sample at least three potential sources of " i+1" material for your specific target language. Your goal is to find things that feel about 70-95% comprehensible.
    • Where to Look:
      • YouTube: Search for "Easy [Language] vlog," "Simple [Language] stories," or "[Language] Comprehensible Input for beginners."
      • Podcasts: If "Coffee Break Languages" felt too easy, try the next season. Or search for "Simple [Language] Podcast."
      • Graded Readers: Search online for "[Language] graded reader A1 sample" or "free easy stories in [Language]." Many sites offer a free chapter or story.
    • How to Test: For each piece of content, listen for 2-3 minutes or read a few paragraphs. Ask yourself: "Can I follow the gist of this without feeling totally lost?" If the answer is yes, it's a candidate!
  3. (15 mins) Build Your Library: Create a new folder in your browser's bookmarks and name it "My Language Library" or something similar. Save the links to the 3+ resources you found that feel like a good "i+1" fit. This is the beginning of your personalized curriculum that you can turn to every day.

You are now not just a learner, but a detective, seeking out the perfect clues to unlock the language. This process of finding your own materials makes the journey much more personal and effective.

When you have bookmarked at least three promising "i+1" resources, move to the next lesson.
