Module Checkpoint

Module 2 Checkpoint: Becoming an Input Detective

30-45 minutes

Excellent. You've now covered all the core concepts of the input phase. You know what kind of material to look for, where to find it, and how to engage with it effectively for both listening and reading.

Now it's time to put it all together and prove to yourself that you can do this.

Your goal for this checkpoint is to demonstrate that you can identify high-quality, "i+1" comprehensible input for yourself out in the wild. This is a crucial skill for becoming an independent and effective language learner.

Your Task: Curate Your Input Diet

Your mission is to find two brand new pieces of content for your target language—one for listening and one for reading. You will then analyze them to confirm they are a good fit for you.

  1. Find One New Audio Resource:
    • Go on a scavenger hunt for a new podcast, YouTube channel, or audio clip that you haven't used before.
    • Listen for a few minutes to gauge the level.
    • It must feel like "i+1"—mostly understandable, but with a little bit of challenge.
  2. Find One New Reading Resource:
    • Find a new short story, graded reader sample, blog post for learners, or simple news article.
    • Read a few paragraphs to gauge the level.
    • It must also feel like "i+1"—you should be able to follow the gist without a dictionary.
  3. Analyze Your Findings:
    • In your language notebook, create a new entry for this checkpoint. For each of the two resources, answer the following questions:
      • Resource: What is the title and where can I find it? (Paste the link or write down the YouTube channel/podcast name).
      • Why is it Comprehensible? What makes it easy to understand? (e.g., "The speaker talks slowly and clearly," "They use lots of visuals and gestures," "The topic is simple and familiar," "It's a story I already know in English").
      • Why is it 'i+1'? What provides the "+1" challenge? (e.g., "I understood the main idea, but they used some new vocabulary for food that I had to guess," "The sentence structure was a bit more complex than I'm used to, but I could still follow").
  4. Review Your Logs:
    • Take a moment to look back at the "Listening Log" and "Reading Log" you started this week. You've already begun to track your journey. This is tangible proof of the work you've put in. These logs will become incredibly motivating to look back on in a few months.

Completing this task proves you have the skills to feed your brain a steady diet of high-quality input, which is the engine of language acquisition.

Once you have found your two new resources and completed your analysis in your notebook, move to the next lesson. You'll then be ready to move on to Module 3, where we'll learn how to systematically memorize the new things you're discovering.
