Module 3: The Memory Machine - Vocabulary & Grammar a Better Way

Building Your Core Vocabulary

Excellent! Your memory machine is online. Having a tool like Soliloquy is like having a superpower, but a superpower is only effective if you know where to point it.

Today we're focusing on the strategy: What exactly should you be putting into this machine to build your language knowledge as efficiently as possible?

Remember the 80/20 rule from Module 1? The idea that a small number of words account for a huge percentage of daily communication? We are now going to combine that powerful strategy with your new powerful tool (Soliloquy).

Your new vocabulary will come from two primary sources, and a healthy routine includes both:

  1. Systematic Input (The "Push" Method): This is where you proactively learn the most important words in the language. You'll use the frequency list you bookmarked in Module 1 as your guide. Instead of learning random words, you will intentionally learn word #1, then #2, then #3, and so on. This builds the essential skeleton of your language knowledge quickly.
  2. Organic Input (The "Pull" Method): This is where you learn words that you personally encounter and find interesting during your Comprehensible Input sessions (your listening and reading). These words make the language feel alive, personal, and relevant to the content you enjoy. This adds the muscle and personality to your skeleton.

A balanced approach is best. Each day, you might decide to learn 5 new words from your frequency list and 5 new words that you discovered in a podcast or story.

How to Create a High-Quality Flashcard:

The quality of your flashcards matters more than the quantity. A great card leads to easy learning; a bad card leads to frustration. Follow these rules:

  • Rule #1: One New Idea Per Card. A flashcard should test you on exactly one piece of information. Don't create a card from a sentence that has three new words in it.
  • Rule #2: Context is King. Never learn isolated words. An isolated word is just a piece of data; a word in a sentence is part of a living language. It shows you grammar, usage, and meaning all at once.
  • Rule #3: If you can't, don't create. When you're a beginner, don't try to make up your own sentences. You will likely make mistakes. Steal sentences directly from your source material (the graded reader, the podcast transcript) or use a reliable source like an online dictionary that provides simple example sentences. Later on you will be able to create your own sentences and get feedback on them with AI.

Putting it all together, a perfect flashcard has the target sentence on the front and the translation (plus maybe a picture for nouns) on the back.

Your 60-Minute Action Plan for Today

  1. (10 mins) Review this Lesson: Solidify the "Systematic + Organic" approach in your mind. This balanced diet is key to building robust vocabulary.
  2. (10 mins) Start Your Daily SRS Habit: This is a new, non-negotiable part of your daily 60 minutes. Always do your scheduled reviews first.
    • Open Soliloquy. It will tell you how many cards are "Due." Click on your deck and complete all your reviews until it says "Congratulations! You have finished this deck for now."
  3. (30 mins) Curate and Add 10 New Cards: Now it's time to add today's new material.
    • Systematic (5 cards): Open your frequency list. Take the next 5 words you need to learn. For each word, find a simple, high-quality example sentence online and create a new Soliloquy card for it.
    • Organic (5 cards): Open your Listening/Reading Log from Module 2. Pick 5 words you logged that you found interesting. Go back to the source material if you can, grab the sentence it was in, and create 5 new Soliloquy cards.
  4. (10 mins) Prepare for Tomorrow: Look at the sentence cards you just made. You'll notice they aren't just teaching you words; they're showing you patterns. "The cat sits," "the dogs sit." You're absorbing grammar without even trying. Our next lesson, "Grammar Without the Grind," will show you how to lean into this natural way of learning patterns.

This daily process of Review > Curate > Add is the engine of your memory machine. It might seem like a small step today, but done consistently, it will lead to an explosion in your vocabulary.

When you've completed your reviews and added your 10 new cards, move to the next lesson.
