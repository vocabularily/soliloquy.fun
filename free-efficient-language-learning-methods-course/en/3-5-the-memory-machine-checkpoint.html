Module Checkpoint

Module 3 Checkpoint: Building the Machine

45-60 minutes

Excellent. You've now explored all the tools and strategies for building a powerful memory system. You have the logical workhorse (SRS) and the creative special forces (mnemonics).

It's time to put all of these skills together in our final checkpoint for this module. This will ensure you are ready to create high-quality learning materials for yourself going forward.

This checkpoint has two parts. First, you'll do a "sprint" to create a foundational set of high-quality flashcards. Second, you'll test your new ability to spot grammatical patterns.

Part 1: The Core 20 Flashcard Challenge

Your mission is to add 20 new, high-quality flashcards to your Soliloquy deck from a frequency list. This exercise will simulate the core process you'll use to build your vocabulary.

  1. Get Your Word List: Open the "Top 100/1000 Most Common Words" frequency list for your language that you bookmarked earlier.
  2. Select Your Targets: Identify the next 20 words on that list that you do not yet know comfortably.
  3. Create Your Cards with a Quality Checklist: For each of the 20 words, create a new Soliloquy card, making sure it meets these criteria:
    • [ ] The Context is King: Is the new word presented in a full, simple sentence?
    • [ ] It's "i+1": Is this the only new word in the sentence?
    • [ ] It's Natural: Did you "steal" this sentence from a reliable source (a graded reader, a dictionary's examples, a podcast transcript) rather than making it up yourself?
    • [ ] The Card is Clear:
      • Front: Full sentence in the target language.
      • Back: Translation of the key word AND the full sentence.
    • [ ] (Bonus Points): Did you add a mnemonic for a tricky word or a picture for a concrete noun?

This is a focused work session. Put on some music and get into a flow. The goal is to internalize the process of creating excellent learning materials.

Part 2: The Grammar Pattern-Spotting Exercise

This exercise tests the skill you learned in Lesson 3.3. Your job is to act like a "pattern detective."

Below are three sentences. Imagine you found them in a text you were reading. Your task is not to translate them, but to identify the primary grammatical pattern being demonstrated in each one. Write your answers in your notebook.

Sentence 1: "The happy child plays with the red ball."

  • What is the main grammar idea this sentence could teach you?

Sentence 2: "I like to read, but I don't like to swim."

  • What is the main grammar idea this sentence could teach you?

Sentence 3: "Today I walk. Yesterday I walked."

  • What is the main grammar idea this sentence could teach you?

(Scroll down for example answers after you've considered your own.)

Example Answers for Part 2

  1. Pattern: The use of adjectives and where they are placed in relation to a noun (e.g., "happy child," "red ball").
  2. Pattern: How to connect two contrasting ideas using a conjunction (like "but").
  3. Pattern: How a verb tense changes to show the difference between present and past action.

Completing this checkpoint demonstrates that you have mastered the core skills of this module. You can systematically and effectively build your own memory bank of vocabulary and grammar.

Now that you have a powerful system for absorbing the language, our next module will focus on the exciting next step: actively using it.

When you have created your 20 flashcards and completed the pattern-spotting exercise, move to the next lesson.
